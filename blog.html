Sarah, Matthew, Marigold, Martyn and Esther I will miss you all, thank you for everything.

Rich for making me a million canvases and supporting me always, thank you.

A huge thank you to Dr Mike Evens who not only supported me through my degree but also into my MA. The advice and insight I have gained from you over the past 4 years of my life has been invaluable.

The Process Behind Historical Sky Paintings

August 15, 2022

Creating my historical sky paintings is a deeply research-intensive process that begins long before brush touches canvas. Each piece requires me to dive into specific moments in history and understand not just what happened on the ground, but what the sky witnessed above.

Using astronomical software like Stellarium, I can recreate the exact star charts from any given date and location in history. This allows me to paint the sky as it truly appeared during pivotal moments – from Charles Darwin's publication of "On the Origin of Species" to the Stonewall Riots.

The juxtaposition between the unchanging beauty of the cosmos and the earth-shattering events below creates a powerful narrative in each painting. While humanity grapples with progress, injustice, and change, the stars remain constant witnesses to our story.

Art as Activism: Why I Paint

June 10, 2022

At 22, I realized I could no longer remain silent about the injustices I witnessed daily. As a white woman, I understand the privilege and responsibility that comes with having a platform and a voice.

My paintings are my form of activism. Each cloud formation, each star chart, each brushstroke is a statement about humanity's capacity for both destruction and beauty. Through my work, I aim to start conversations about climate change, social justice, and our shared responsibility to create a better world.

The natural disasters series, in particular, serves as a stark reminder of our planet's fragility and the urgent need for environmental action. When I paint Hurricane Maria or the Australian bushfires, I'm not just capturing atmospheric data – I'm documenting the consequences of our collective choices.
